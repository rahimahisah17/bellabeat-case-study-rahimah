About the Company

Urka Sren and Sando Mur founded Bellabeat, a high-tech company that manufactures health-focused smart products for women. Collecting data on activity, sleep, stress, and reproductive health has allowed Bellabeat to empower women with knowledge about their own health and habits. Bellabeat is a successful small company, but they have the potential to become a larger player in the global smart device market. Urka Sren, cofounder and Chief Creative Officer of Bellabeat, believes that there are new growth opportunities for the company.

Business Task

Analyze smart device usage data to uncover user activity, identify key wellness behaviors, and generate insights that will guide Bellabeats marketing strategies aimed at improving customer engagement and expanding market reach.

Summary Insights

  • There are more lightly active users than very active users, and the largest category is the sedentary users.

  • The average Total steps is 7281 which does not meet the popular goal of 10,000 steps. The WHO supports research that indicates that at least 7,000 steps/day are associated with a lower risk of early death and cardiovascular disease. While 10,000 steps is a popular goal, studies suggest that many of the significant health benefits start to be seen at around 7,000 steps.

  • The output is a matrix table that shows correlation between each pair. Correlation values range from -1 to +1: +1 means perfect positive correlation(as one increases, the other also increases). 0 means no correlation i.e no relationship and -1 shows perfect negative correlation(as one increases, the other decreases).

  • A correlation of 0.6 between TotalSteps and Calories is a significant positive correlation in human behavior where so many factors. It means the more steps taken, the more calories burned (strong relationship).

  • A correlation of -0.2 between VeryActiveMinutes and SedentaryMinutes is a negative correlation, telling us that the two variables dont move together in the same direction. It means the more active minutes a user has, the less sedentary time he has. This makes perfect sense.

  • And a correlation of 0.98 between TotalSteps and TotalDistance is a near perfection relationship. This is expected because the more distance is covered when more steps are taken.

Recommendation

Here are some strategic recommendations directly tied to the findings of the analysis:

  1. Introduce daily activity reminders, promote health reminders or mindfulness features since many users have 0-step days. Encourage wearing the device daily through wear reminders or Lets start your day notifications.

  2. Promote step-based challenges, since users hover around 4k10k steps. Launch weekly step challenges, badges, and streak rewards to increase engagement. This can push users from moderate to higher activity. Emphasize motivation: Take small steps toward big goals.

  3. Improve onboarding to reduce zero-activity days. Zero-step days often mean users forgot to sync or charge the device. Add onboarding tips like How to get the most out of Bellabeat and dynamic charge reminders.

  4. Personal activity guidance. Since most users dont hit high-step targets. Provide personalized daily goals rather than a default 10,000-step target. Marketing should emphasize Bellabeats ability to help women reach daily movement goals and track progress easily.

  5. The device data is consistent physical activity (steps, minutes) strongly relates to energy output (calories). Users who move more are indeed healthier/more active suggesting Bellabeat can market activity tracking accuracy and health insights as key value points. And the strong negative relationship between active and sedentary minutes highlights a potential opportunity for Bellabeat to promote features that help users reduce inactivity such as personalized movement reminders or goal-based notifications. Bellabeat can emphasize precision and trustworthiness in tracking.

  6. The data shows that most users are moderately active, averaging between 7,0008,000 steps per day. The majority of daily time is spent sedentary, with fewer than 30 minutes of very active movement. This suggests that Bellabeat users tend to use their devices more for health monitoring than intensive fitness tracking. Marketing can leverage these insights by promoting Bellabeat as a wellness companion focusing on gentle habit-building, mindfulness reminders, and personalized daily goals to encourage consistent movement throughout the day.

  7. Since sedentary time is high for many users, Bellabeat could introduce gentle movement reminders to encourage activity. This is an opportunity to promote movement reminders and micro-activity challenges.
